HOW DOES IT WORK?

The dark web lurks beneath the surface of the internet, hidden from conventional search engines and accessible only through special software like Tor. It's a shadowy realm where anonymity reigns, masking users' identities and locations. Here, encrypted websites house a maze of illicit activities—drug trafficking, weapons deals, stolen data markets, and more. Unlike the surface web, which is easily accessible, the dark web thrives on secrecy, with its onion-like structure allowing users to remain hidden. But beware—venturing too deep can lead to dangerous encounters, as this hidden world is filled with both deception and lurking threats.
